Wolanski, John Jozef

Birth Name Wolanski, John Jozef
Gender male
Age at Death 56 years, 8 months, 14 days

Narrative

John served in the 163rd Infantry from enlistment until September 14, 1918. He then served in the 126th Infantry from Sept. 14, 1918 until discharge in May 1919.

His assignments likely meant that he fought on the front lines, in the trenches of WW1. His military service record states that he was assigned to units that occupied German territory.

Family members (Marian Thomas: grand niece (John's sister's granddaughter)) recalls that her mother (Angela Naber (John's niece)) remember that he was strongly impacted from his military service. His service record doesn't declare an injury, but John struggled the remainder of his life due to his experiences in WW1. At one point he was put in charge of the family tavern, but then struggled with alcoholism issues and was removed from this job.
